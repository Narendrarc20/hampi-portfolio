World's Historic Place Hampi

Hampi is a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Karnataka, India. It was the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire, one of the greatest kingdoms in Indian history, flourishing between the 14th and 16th centuries.

Explore Hampi's Marvels

Discover the rich history and stunning architecture of the Vijayanagara Empire.

Virupaksha Temple

One of the oldest and most famous temples in Hampi, dedicated to Lord Shiva. It features a towering gopuram, intricate carvings, and a pillared hall.

Vittala Temple

Renowned for its extraordinary architecture, including the iconic Stone Chariot and musical pillars that resonate with sound when tapped.

Lotus Mahal

An exquisite structure known for its unique blend of Indo-Islamic architecture. Shaped like a lotus flower, it served as a recreational pavilion.
